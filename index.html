My Favorite Activity


My favorite hobby is to play bass with my band, Anticline. We are a Harrisonburg, VA based DIY indie/pop/punk band. Anticline started in January 2024 and has 5 members. We write our own songs and currently have 8 originals that we play. Our song, “I Like Him!” will be featured in the SMAD short film, “Eclipse” on May 7th at SMADfest. We love performing and have played at Crayola, JMU GIVE’s Lei-down, Kappa Pi’s Art-fest, and Dude Ranch.

I started playing bass in May 2022 and joined Project Serenity, the first band I ever played with, that June. We played many gigs in Virginia and even JMU’s 2023 Purple Reign! We were lucky enough to be featured in JMU’s newspaper, The Breeze, as well.

Playing with a band has given me many amazing opportunities and improved my skills as a musician and a performer. My two basses, amplifier, and effects pedals are my prized possessions!

The First Bass I Tried


The first bass I ever played was a Squire J Affinity Bass Guitar in blue. My friend let me use it over the summer to see if I enjoyed playing. This 4-string is an affordable beginner level bass. It is made from poplar and maple wood, has two single-coil Jazz Bass pickups, and roundwound strings. This bass was relatively reliable. It was played for many years and had no issues until a small electrical problem when I used it.

The First Bass I Owned


During 2022 summer I got my first bass for my birthday! It is a Sterling by Music Man StingRay in Olive . I went to my local Guitar Center and tested lots of basses out and this was my favorite within my budget. It is a step above a beginner bass and is good quality. The tone of this instrument is very versatile and is powered with 2 humbucker active pickups. I really enjoy this bass. The only issue is there buzzing from the strings I cannot fix without professionally straightening the neck of the bass.

My Current Bass


Over the 2023-2024 Winter break I went to Chuck Levin’s Music Center , our favorite music store, with my dad to (originally) window shop before I went back to school. There I saw the Spector Professional Series Rex Brown Signature Bass in Holoflash Black and I fell in love with it. The sound and feel are much more professional than my Music Man bass and the holographic color is super cool on stage. It has 2 active pickups, a solid maple body, and a beautiful rosewood fingerboard. Rex Brown does not make this bass anymore, so it feels very special and unique! This bass is also a bit bigger with 24 rather than the average 21 frets. It has flatwound strings which makes it easier to play in my opinion. After I got this bass, something just clicked, and I feel like I became a better musician overnight.

My Amplifier


My grandparents got me my Rumble 100 Bass Amplifier to go with my first bass during 2022 summer. It has an awesome sound and is very loud for its weight, 22 lbs, and size, 18.5” x 16.5” x 14”. The controls include a 4-band EQ, drive, overdrive level, master volume, bright switch, mid-scoop contour switch, and vintage switch. This amp has been to every show of time and gotten thrown around a bit and it still works like it is brand new. The only issue is that sometimes it is not loud enough for outdoor shows or venues where the crowd is loud.

My Pedals


I have three effects pedals. The first one is an Aguilar Storm King distortion/fuzz pedal. The pedal is very small but packs a huge punch! The shape and gain dials produce a variety of tones, distortion, and fuzz. My second pedal, the KLIQ Tiny-Compressor Effect Pedal is one I'm still figuring out how to successfully use. The compression difference when using this pedal seems minimal; however, I am still experimenting with it. My third pedal, the Electro-Harmonix Big Muff Bass Fuzz Pedal creates a huge, bold sound. I use it anytime I play a punk song.

Bass Upgrade


I won't be upgrading my bass anytime soon because the current one I use is everything I want and need. However, if the time to upgrade comes I would look at the Ibanez SR2600 Premium Bass in Cerulean Blue Burst. This bass is absolutely gorgeous and professional grade. On this bass, one can adjust the string spacing which is a great feature. The layers of Panga Panga / Maple / Paduak / Purpleheart / Walnut wood makes the neck very stable and is a visually appealing detail as well. I would love to play this bass!

Amplifier Upgrade


Unlike a bass upgrade, I actually need an amp upgrade to be loud enough at shows. My next amp will probably be the Fender Rumble 500 . This amp looks the same as the Rumble 100 but is larger and much louder. The 500W's of power would be loud enough for any gig.

Pedal Upgrade


If I were to get a new pedal, I would get the Fender Downtown Express Bass Multi-Effect Pedal . This pedal has compression, 3-band EQ, and overdrive- everything that I would need! It replaces the need for multiple pedals which is very convenient.
